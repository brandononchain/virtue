INT. COFFEE SHOP - MORNING

A quiet corner booth. Rain streaks the window. JAMES, 40s, unshaven, stares into a cold cup of coffee. SARAH, 30s, slides into the seat across from him.

SARAH
You look terrible.

JAMES
Thanks. I haven't slept.

SARAH
Since when?

JAMES
Since the hearing. Since they showed the footage.

SARAH
James, that footage doesn't prove anything.

JAMES
It proves I was there. It proves I saw what happened and I didn't stop it.

Sarah reaches across the table and takes his hand.

SARAH
(gentle)
You couldn't have known. Nobody could have known.

JAMES
(pulling away)
Then why do I keep seeing it? Every time I close my eyes — the fire, the faces, the sound of —

He stops. His jaw tightens. Sarah watches him, eyes glistening.

SARAH
(barely audible)
Come home, James. Please.

A long silence. Rain on glass. James finally looks at her.

JAMES
I don't know how to be the person you need me to be anymore.
