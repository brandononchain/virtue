EXT. ROOFTOP - NIGHT

KAI sprints across the rain-slicked rooftop. Behind him, three DRONES cut through the fog, red tracking lasers sweeping the darkness.

Kai reaches the edge. Eight stories down. No way across.

A drone fires. The shot shatters concrete at his feet. Kai dives right, rolls, comes up running parallel to the ledge.

He spots a construction crane extending over the gap to the next building. The arm sways in the wind.

KAI
(under his breath)
Terrible idea.

He jumps onto the crane arm. Metal groans. The drones swarm around him, recalibrating. Kai runs along the arm as it tilts — one step, two, three —

He leaps.

For a frozen moment he's suspended in rain and searchlight.

He crashes through a glass skylight, hits a steel catwalk, tumbles, grabs a railing. Hangs. Below, a vast server room hums with blue light.

The drones circle the building, unable to follow. Their red lights sweep the broken skylight overhead.

Kai pulls himself up. Blood on his hands. He looks down at the server room. Rows and rows of processors. This is it. The archive.

KAI
(panting)
Found you.
